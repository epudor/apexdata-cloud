**A large language model (LLM)** is an AI model trained on vast amounts of text to understand and generate human language. LLMs power tools like OpenAI's GPT, Anthropic's Claude, and Google's Gemini.

## Why they matter

LLMs can write, summarize, translate, classify, reason over text, and answer questions — making them the engine of modern [generative AI](/glossary/generative-ai/). For business use, they're typically combined with [RAG](/glossary/retrieval-augmented-generation/) to ground them in private, current data.

## Key concepts

LLMs have a *training cutoff* (they don't know recent or private information by default), can *hallucinate* (state false things confidently), and vary in cost, latency, and capability. Choosing and grounding the right model for a task is a core part of [generative AI consulting](/services/generative-ai-consulting/).
